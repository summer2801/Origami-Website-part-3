Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  3. Camels can reach 7 feet in height (at the hump) and weigh up to 1500 pounds.

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are a part of iguana suborder
  2. They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
  3. A chameleon’s foot consists of two fleshy pads that oppose each other. One pad contains three digits that are fused together while the other has two fused digits.

Teddy

  1. A teddy bear is a stuffed toy in the form of a bear.
  2. The word or name ‘teddy bear’ comes from the nickname of Theodore Roosevelt.
